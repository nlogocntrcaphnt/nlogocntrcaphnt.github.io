Dimitris Liantinis (/ˌliːənˈtiːnɪs/; Greek: Δημήτρης Λιαντίνης [ʎa(n)ˈdinis]; born 23 July 1942, disappeared 1 June 1998) was a Greek philosopher. He was associate professor at the Department of Pedagogy of the Faculty of Philosophy, Pedagogy and Psychology of the University of Athens, teaching the course "Philosophy of Education and Teaching of Greek Language and Literature" A great lover of Ancient Greek culture, he devoted his life in studying and reinterpreting their cultural heritage. He wrote on various philosophical issues, including education, morality and death."

Liantinis took his own life on the 1st of June, 1998. The full extent of his intent had only been revealed to his cousin (and best friend), whom he had made swear an oath of secrecy concerning the location of his "voluntary departure", as he'd call it. In a letter addressed to his daughter where he writes:

"..I am leaving by my own volition. Disappearing while still standing
on my own two feet, firm and proud."

The letter was found, sealed in a plastic bag, alongside a lighter, a packet of cigs and a yet unused vile of poison, in the cave where Liantinis carefully chose for his departure from this world.
